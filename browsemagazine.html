We helped Browse split their product into two parts - a monthly print magazine that would cover the big stories of the past month with a brand new website to cover breaking news and promote the upcoming editions of the magazine.


The Company

Browse is the only monthly independent culture magazine based in Kingston Upon Hull. It is available both online and in print, and showcases and promotes the landscape as the city approaches 2017. Browse creates an accessible brand that appeals to the people of Hull. Their digital version has been well received across the city.


The Brief

Browse Magazine was an online only magazine that was released roughly every month. The brief was to help develop their online presence, increase engagement on social media, and help them make the move to a physical print magazine. The secondary goals were to develop the brand and content, bringing everything more in line so that the brand could move forward with a strong, consistent image.


Digital Strategy

We began by developing the existent digital content and marketing of the online magazine. We helped them reach a milestone of achieving 100,000 pageviews, generating much of the interest via Facebook where we saw 84% of users linking from the page. This set up the foundations for us to move to the second stage of the strategy. The analytics showed that whilst the magazine was now receiving a great readership and engagement on social media, this was confined to the time around the release of each issue. With the goal of moving to a monthly print magazine in mind, we developed a blog-based website that would allow the team to post news throughout the month whilst hyping the upcoming print edition. This would keep the readers interested and keep the brand relevant throughout the month, not just around the release of each magazine. The website caused the engagement and readership of the magazine to even out across the month, keeping people coming back daily to check for new content with the following on social media growing by 15% each month.



Print Strategy

With the following eagerly checking back for more content and the social media account seeing constant engagement from fans, we began work on the initial print copy of the magazine. We developed a prototype and a media pack to help get advertisers on board with the project. The magazine received a positive reception with many advertisers signing up for space from all around the city. We then co-ordinated with the Browse team to help refine the content for the upcoming print edition and went to work designing the final product. The magazine went to print with 5,000 copies being produced in time for distribution at a festival that saw thousands of people from all over the city in attendance.
