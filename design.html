CMS


We understand that having a website is one thing but having a website with which you can interact and make changes is often the key point of having a web presence. Many clients choose to keep us on a retainer to make changes month to month, but it there are a few that require the ability to update the site themselves. Doing this typically requires a Content Management System (CMS). This system is essentially an interface that allows you to fill in a few fields and then voila! Your page has been updated. One of the most popular publishers of this sort is Wordpress. It may have started as a mere blogging platform, but it has quickly become one of the most popular web platforms in the world. In fact, Wordpress accounts for around 20% of all websites on the Internet today. If you're looking to make frequent updates yourself, a CMS may be the way to go.

Responsive Design


It's no use having a website that looks beautiful on a desktop but falls apart completely when it comes to viewing it on a mobile phone. Similarly, there's no point having separate desktop and mobile sites in an attempt to cover all the bases. The more elegant and modern solution is to configure the site so that it knows how to respond to the screen it is being displayed on. Our team ensure that all of our sites are responsive as standard - the world is moving too quickly for you to have to make do with a site that a growing portion of your audience can't use. With a growing number of people who view the web exclusively on a mobile platform, it is becoming so important to make sure that you're not leaving them having to do that awkward reverse pinch gesture just to read your content.

Digital Marketing


Back when we were typing away on those old computers with no user interface other than lines of green text, companies marketed themselves in a very different way. Much of it revolved around creating a strong presence in the targeted community on the high street and this often involved a lot of print work and news coverage.
Since the advent of the digital age, well... not a lot has changed. When you take away all the buzz words and fancy phrases the goal is ultimately the same. Digital marketing strongly focuses on building a community or following and promoting your brand to them. However, with the powerful tool that is social media and the dominance of smartphones, the methods have evolved. It is now possible to put your brand into the pocket of every person in your demographic, putting your services and products a mere click away.

Analytics


Along with constantly evolving methods, digital marketing can give us insights into your viewer/reader/customer's browsing habits like never before. Could you imagine, 30 years ago, having a catalogue full of your products which you could access to find out which sections are receiving the most attention? Utilising analytics reports, we can monitor the online habits of everyone that visits you online. If you're seeing plenty of traffic and engagement in some areas but little to none in others it tells us invaluable information that we can use to help refine your digital strategy. Similarly we can gain useful information on who is visiting you, where they are hearing about you and where they are visiting you from. This information is so helpful in ensuring that you're targeting the demographics that you need to be targeting; if that isn't the case, there's plenty we can do to help you shift your strategy.

SEO


One of the most prevalent methods of forming a digital strategy lies in your Search Engine Optimisation. The way we like to describe this to our clients is as so: Imagine you've gone to a party where you know nobody. Your aim at this party is to make new friends, connections and learn a bit about the people there so you can learn their likes and dislikes. The best way to achieve your goal is with a lot of talking, telling people about yourself and, most importantly, using the right words. SEO isn't much different. Your content contains everything that search engines know about you and your company; if you use all the right words and portray yourself correctly, when a user searches for something related to your company the search engine effectively says "Hey! I know a guy I met at a party that does that!" In this case you are the guy, the party is the internet and the search engine is that popular prom king or queen who knows everybody

Social Media


Your target demographic are (unless you know something we don't) just people. They use the internet like people. They talk like people. They make connections like people. It is so important that you communicate to your audience on a personal level. Chances are that upwards of 90% of your demographic are using some form of Social Media. From Facebook and Twitter to Tumblr and Pinterest to MSN and Myspace (Just kidding! No one uses those last two any more!), your audience are out there tweeting, sharing, communicating. It makes sense then, for your company to build your brand in a place that people can easily communicate with you. Why build a strong web presence out in the sticks where no one will see it, when there is plenty of prime, available space on social networks? The concept of build it and they will come" is all well and good, but when you have unparalleled access to your audience's social circles, is that not something worth working with?
